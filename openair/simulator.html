How it works

1. Demand per room

Each room computes a demand (0–1) for CO₂ and humidity independently: (value − target) / (max − target), clamped to [0, 1]. The room's demand is the higher of the two. Demand is smoothed over time with an exponential moving average (EMA), controlled by EMA alpha — higher alpha means faster response.

2. Fan speed

Fan demand equals the highest room demand, then EMA-smoothed. A single bad room is enough to spin the fan up. Fan speed is mapped linearly from Fan min to Fan max based on this demand.

3. Valve positions

Each valve opens proportionally to its room's demand relative to the fan's normalised demand: valve = valve_min + (room_demand / fan_demand) × (valve_max − valve_min). Rooms that caused the fan to spin up get fully open valves; rooms with low demand get the minimum opening.
